Gas Stations & General Stores

Places to fuel up both vehicles and humans:

  • Big Sur Deli & General Store — central Big Sur; food, drinks, souvenirs, and gasoline.
  • Big Sur River Inn General Store & Gas — gas, snacks, and river-side hangout.
  • Fernwood General Store — convenience items, camping supplies, espresso, beer & wine.
  • Ripplewood Resort Store & Chevron — groceries, basic camping gear, small store and gas.
  • Gorda Springs Resort / Gorda By The Sea Mini Mart — gas, mini-mart, and ocean views in south Big Sur.

Restaurants & Cafés

Food with views (and occasionally with sticker shock):

  • Nepenthe & Café Kevah — classic cliff-top restaurant and café overlooking the coast.
  • Big Sur Lodge Restaurant — tucked in Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park among redwoods.
  • Big Sur River Inn Restaurant — meals by the fireplace or out by the river chairs.
  • Big Sur Roadhouse — casual California cuisine with local ingredients.
  • Treebones Resort – The Grill & Sunset Sushi Bar — ocean-view dining in south Big Sur.

Lodging & Inns

From cabins in the redwoods to cliff-top luxury:

  • Big Sur Lodge — cabins and rooms inside Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park.
  • Big Sur River Inn — historic inn with riverside lawn and motel-style rooms.
  • Glen Oaks Big Sur — modern cabins and cottages among redwoods.
  • Deetjen’s Big Sur Inn — historic, storybook-style inn in Castro Canyon.
  • Post Ranch Inn — luxury adults-only resort high above the ocean.
  • Ventana Big Sur — resort plus glamping tucked into the hillsides.
  • Gorda Springs Resort — ocean-view cottages, restaurant, and gas in the hamlet of Gorda.
  • Treebones Resort — yurts and “glamping” overlooking the Pacific.

Campgrounds

For tents, RVs, and cabin-style camping:

  • Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park Campground — large campground along the Big Sur River.
  • Big Sur Campground & Cabins — redwoods, river access, RV and tent sites, plus cabins.
  • Riverside Campground & Cabins — shady tent and RV sites along the river.
  • Fernwood Campground & Resort — mix of tent sites, cabins, and adventure tents.
  • Andrew Molera State Park — walk-in environmental campsites near the river and beach.
  • Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park — limited environmental campsites with dramatic views.
  • Limekiln State Park — redwoods, streams, and oceanfront campsites.

State Parks & Beaches

Key public lands and shorelines along the route:

  • Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park — redwood canyon, river, and many hiking trails (no direct beach access).
  • Andrew Molera State Park — meadows, river, and a long walk-in beach.
  • Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park — home of McWay Falls and protected marine reserve.
  • Garrapata State Park & Beach — coastal bluffs, trails, and a rugged state beach.
  • Limekiln State Park — historic lime kilns, redwoods, and small cove beach.
  • Pfeiffer Beach — iconic purple sand and Keyhole Rock (USFS access road off Highway 1).
  • Sand Dollar Beach — wide sandy beach and surf spot south of Big Sur village.
